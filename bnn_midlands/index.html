Updates in Bancroft-Covington Case

BNN has exclusive new details regarding the alleged corruption scandal currently unfolding in Midlands. Recall that, BNN was first to bring you the report that both Chase Covington and Avery Bancroft are facing bribery charges. Bancroft allegedly bribed Covington in order to obtain a casino license.

BNN has exclusively learned that Bancroft's attorneys have filed an intent to offer an affirmative defense of entrapment with the Polk County District Court. Asked to comment, a spokesperson for the Polk County Prosecutor's office stated, "This is a last-ditch attempt by a desperate defendant to muddy the waters and obscure the issues. The evidence is clear: Avery Bancroft is a criminal."

We look forward to explaining this to jurors in the coming months."

    Prosecutors To Be Hired

    Mayor Heisman, citing an unusual increase in crime that seems to occur every other year in Midlands, suggested to the City Council on April 1 that the city should begin hiring prosecutors, to start August 15th.

  • Back To School!

    Art Therapy Offered

    Shannon Ellis, 5th grade teacher at Fairview Middle School, stopped by BNN to share some back to school news. "We're going to have art therapy for all of the children this year," she said. "The students will draw comics, make sculptures, and do charcoal drawings about their feelings and how to make friends." Ellis says the school district hopes this new program will make the little school less of an overt war zone.

  • Vows

    Lyons-Kaminsky Engagement

    Charlie Kaminsky and Sam Lyons announce their upcomming marriage. Kaminsky drives limosines and sedans for AAAA Luxury Transportation in Midlands. Lyons is a cab driver for AAA Cab Company -- "The First Name in Personal Transportation Services." We wish them much happiness.

  • School Board Elections

    Midori Announces Candidacy
