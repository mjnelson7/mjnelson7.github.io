Bancroft and Covington Indicted

Midlands Attorney General Ryan Sullivan announced today that a Grand Jury has indicted both Chase Covington and Avery Bancroft on bribery charges. Covington is the Chair of Midlands Gambling Commission. Bancroft allegedly bribed Covington in order to obtain a casino license.

Sullivan said, "This kind of backroom dealing must be stopped. This is the first step toward rooting out corruption in an agency that has long worked for the highest bidder rather than the people of this great state."

As reported earlier, the arrests were the result of a sting operation. New information has revealed that an unnamed individual working for Bancroft assisted an undercover police officer in the investigation. At this time, it is unknown whether the Bancroft employee was also implicated in the alleged crime.

Both Covington and Bancroft have declined to comment on their indictment.

    Nursing Home Opens

    Citing demographic trends, a new nursing home opened in Midlands May 1st.
