  Welcome! I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Political Science at Washington University in St. Louis where I am also a Graduate Student Associate in the Center for Empirical Research in the Law. My primary interests are American political institutions, with particular attention to state judicial politics. My second field is quantitative methods, and I also study comparative judicial politics. Before coming to Washington University, I received a B.A. in Political Science and English with a concentration in Business Law from Drake University in Des Moines, Iowa.

My dissertation research examines the effects that state methods of judicial selection and retention have on the law state judges use to ground their opinions and the language they use to describe their reasoning. I am also working on projects involving judicial selection mechanisms worldwide, the politics of criminal justice and public opinion, and the confirmation of U.S. attorneys.
