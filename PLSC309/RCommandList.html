PL SC 309: R Commands

We need to load the foreign library to load the commands that R needs to open outside data files. Then we use read.dta() or read.csv() to open the data file:

library(foreign)
setwd("~/Dropbox/QPA/Data/2012ANES")
anes <- read.dta("2012ANES.dta")

**attach(anes)** tells R to learn the names the variables in your dataset. If you do not attach your data, you must type datasetName$variableName every time you reference a variable. If you do not attach() your dataset, you must type anes$ every time you reference a variable.

table(anes$voted2008,anes$voted2012)

##                  No, Didnt Vote Yes, Voted
##   No, Didnt Vote            758        440
##   Yes, Voted                341       3954

sum() adds together all of the values of a vector. Note that we have to add na.rm=TRUE if we have missing values in our vector.

sum(x=c(4, 5, 6, 8, 18))

## [1] 41

sum(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8))

## [1] NA

sum(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8), na.rm=TRUE)

## [1] 26

mean() tells us the average of a vector. Note that na.rm=TRUE also works for this command.

mean(x=c(4, 5, 6, 8, 18))

## [1] 8.2

mean(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8))

## [1] NA

mean(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8), na.rm=TRUE)

## [1] 6.5

median() tells us the median value of a vector. Note that na.rm=TRUE also works for this command.

median(x=c(4, 5, 6, 8, 18))

## [1] 6

median(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8))

## [1] NA

median(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8), na.rm=TRUE)

## [1] 6.5

quantile() gives us a specified quantile of a vector. Note that na.rm=TRUE also works for this command. To get the median (the .5 quantile) of our vector:

quantile(x=c(5, 6, 7, NA, 8), probs=.5, na.rm=TRUE)

## 50% 
## 6.5

Graphing Data

hist() allows us to make histograms.

hist(anes$age, xlab="Age of Survey Respondents", main="Distribution of Ages", col="orange", breaks=15)

plot() makes scatterplotplots.

plot(x=anes$age, y=anes$pctWillVote, main="Likelihood of Voting, by Age", xlab="Respondent's Age",ylab="Percent Likelihood of Voting", pch=3, col="orange")

boxplot() makes boxplots.

boxplot(anes$age~anes$snl, main="Age, by SNL Viewership", xlab="Watch SNL?")

Working with Data

For our example, we need to create two datasets. Let's use the 2008 and 2012 Republican and Democratic vote in six states:

merge() combines datasets. The by="state" part of the command tells R what common variable to use to join observations.

results2012 <- data.frame(
     state = I(c("Alabama","Alaska","Arizona","Arkansas","California","Colorado")),
     obama2012pct = c(36.38,40.81,44.59,36.88,60.24,51.49),
     romney2012pct = c(60.55,54.80,53.65,60.57,37.12,46.13))
results2008 <- data.frame(
     state = I(c("Alabama","Alaska","Arizona","Arkansas","California","Colorado")),
     obama2008pct = c(38.74,37.89,45.12,38.86,61.01,53.66),
     mccain2008pct = c(60.32,59.42,53.64,58.72,36.95,44.71))   
m1 <- merge(results2012, results2008, by = "state")
